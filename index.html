The Museum

A Century of Stories

From the feverish tin rush of the 1880s to the cultural tapestry woven by Hakka miners, British colonists, and Sikh immigrants — 22 Hale Street holds the memories this city was built upon.

Our restored Edwardian shophouse spans five floors of permanent and rotating collections, each room a doorway into a different chapter of Ipoh's remarkable becoming.

The History of Hale Street

From tin rush to heritage site — a century of stories etched into stone, teak, and memory.

1880s
The Tin Rush Begins
The Kinta Valley is revealed as one of the richest tin deposits on earth. Thousands of Hakka and Cantonese miners flood into what will become Ipoh.
The Building is Constructed
A prosperous Hakka merchant commissions the shophouse at Hale Street, blending colonial Edwardian architecture with Straits Chinese sensibility.
1920s
1941
The Japanese Occupation
Ipoh falls under Japanese occupation. The shophouse shelters multiple families during three turbulent years. Hidden documents survive within its walls.
Independence & New Beginnings
Merdeka brings new energy to Ipoh. The building passes through several hands — a trading house, then a family home — through the boom years.
1957
2010s
The Heritage Project
A dedicated team of preservationists begins the painstaking restoration of 22 Hale Street, recovering original floors, plasterwork, and over 3,500 artifacts.
Doors Open to the World
22 Hale Street Heritage Centre opens its doors, welcoming visitors from across Malaysia and the world to experience Ipoh's living memory.
